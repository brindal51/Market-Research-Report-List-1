<p><p>Microactuators are essential components in various applications, driven by different mechanisms. Electrostatic microactuators utilize electric charges to create movement, while electromagnetic types harness magnetic fields. Piezoelectric microactuators generate motion through material deformation under electrical voltage. Fluid microactuators operate via fluid motion, typically in biomedical applications, and thermal microactuators use temperature changes to initiate movement. Each type has unique advantages, making them suitable for specific applications in fields like robotics, telecommunications, and medical devices.</p></p>
