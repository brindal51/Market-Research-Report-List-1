<p><p>The automotive active steering system market is segmented into Active Front Axle Steering and Active Rear Axle Steering. Active Front Axle Steering enhances maneuverability and stability by adjusting the front wheel angles based on speed and driving conditions. This improves handling during tight turns and high-speed driving. Active Rear Axle Steering, on the other hand, allows the rear wheels to turn in the opposite direction to the front wheels at low speeds for improved agility, and in the same direction at high speeds to enhance stability, optimizing vehicle control dynamically.</p></p>
